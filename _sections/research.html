3D Simulation of Synthetic Aperture Sonar (SAS) Imagery
05/2019 – 06/2019 & 11/2019 - Present

This project is dedicated to develop a simulator to generate simulated SAS imagery using OpenGL and Qt.
At first we developed a demo of Side Scan Sonar simulator (as displayed on the right), I built the OpenGL part of the simulator.
Now we are working on the SAS simulator, I also built the OpenGL part of the SAS simulator.
Currently me and my fellow are working the final imaging part of the project.

Internship at Beijing Ensonic Acoustic Technology Company
07/2019 – 09/2019

Participated in the development of an Acoustic Camera product.
Acoustic Camera is basically a microphone array with a camera, and used for sound scource localization.
I investigated and surveyed a few novel source localization algorithms,
and implemented MUSIC (Multiple Signal Classification), MVDR (Minimum Variance Distortionless Response) and GSC (Generalized Sidelobe Canceller) algorithms in C++.

Research on Synthetic Aperture Sonar Image Segmentation
12/2018 - 09/2019

Proposed an improved RSF (Region-Scalable Fitting) model with adaptive kernel function’s scale to segment SAS images.
Proposed an initialization method based on K-means to initialize level set functions.
Submitted one paper “Segmentation method for synthetic aperture sonar image using RSF model with adaptive kernel function’s scale” to ACTA ACUSTICA in late September. Still under reviewing.

A DIY Synthesizer with an 8-steps Sequencer
06/2016 - 07/2016

I built this simple synthesizer in university (as displayed on the right).
The NO.1 part can generate square wave, and with 19 switches as keys. Then a photoresistance is used to change the pitch when coverd.
The NO.2 part is a 8-steps sequencer using 8 relays controlled by a STC89C51 single chip.
My classmate did part of the soldering work and wrote the program for the STC89C51 single chip.
